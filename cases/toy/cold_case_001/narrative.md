# Cold Case 001 — Carnaden Antiques Shop Killing

## Background

On the morning of Monday 7 November 2022, Robert ("Robbie") Thomson, part-time
assistant at Drummond Antiques on Carnaden High Street, arrived to open the
shop at 09:30. He found the rear storeroom door unlocked and the body of the
shop's owner, Ewan Drummond (52), inside. Mr Drummond had suffered a single
severe blow to the back of the head.

Ewan Drummond was a long-term resident of Carnaden, a village of approximately
1,800 people in the Scottish Borders, around 25 miles south of Edinburgh. He
had run Drummond Antiques for eighteen years following his divorce from
Marion Drummond (49), now resident in Galashiels. The shop dealt primarily in
18th- and 19th-century Scottish silverware, jewellery, and small furniture.

## Discovery and initial examination

The post-mortem, conducted by Dr Iain MacLeod at Edinburgh Royal Infirmary,
established cause of death as a single blunt-force injury to the occipital
region. No defensive injuries were found, suggesting Mr Drummond did not see
his attacker. Time of death was estimated between 21:00 on Friday 4 November
and 03:00 on Saturday 5 November — the broad window reflecting the cool
ambient temperature of the unheated storeroom.

The shop's main and rear doors were intact, with no signs of forced entry.
The rear door was found unlocked on the morning of discovery; the front
shutters had been pulled down externally, consistent with someone leaving via
the rear after locking down the front.

A stock-take conducted on Saturday afternoon by attending officers and
Mr Thomson identified missing items totalling approximately £4,445 in value:
a Victorian silver tea service, a small jewellery box, and a silver-handled
letter opener.

## Persons of interest

Three individuals were identified during the initial investigation:

**Calum Sinclair (52)**, a silent investor and 30% owner of the shop, based
in Edinburgh. Mr Sinclair had been in dispute with Mr Drummond for some
months regarding a proposal to sell the premises to a café chain. The two
had spoken by telephone at 18:47 on the evening of the incident.

**Marion Drummond (49)**, the deceased's ex-wife. The Drummonds divorced in
2018; Ms Drummond had a financial settlement claim outstanding at the time
of death. She retained keys to the shop from the period of their marriage.

**Robbie Thomson (28)**, the shop's part-time assistant for six months at
the time of the incident, and the person who discovered the body. Resident
in Carnaden. Some history of minor offending in adolescence; no recent
convictions.

## Forensic findings

Forensic recovery from the scene was limited. A single partial fingerprint
was recovered from the rear door frame which did not match the victim, the
assistant Mr Thomson, the ex-wife Ms Drummond, or the business partner
Mr Sinclair (the latter three providing samples voluntarily). The print
returned no match in any database accessed.

The murder weapon was not recovered. Examination of the wound pattern is
consistent with a heavy blunt object approximately 4–5 cm wide with a
rounded edge. The shop contained several items that could have produced
such a wound; none were identified as missing or showed evidence of having
been used.

## Status

The case was reviewed at six and eighteen months by the Major Investigation
Team and no further leads were developed. It was formally classified as a
cold case in May 2024. The structured evidence catalogue is in
`evidence.json`. The designated "intended" interpretation for evaluation
purposes is held in `ground_truth.md` and must not be consulted during
system runs.
